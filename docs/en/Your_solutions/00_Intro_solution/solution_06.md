# Task 06 - Function Analysis

## Problem Statement

Consider the function

$$
f(x) = 3x^2 - 12x + 7
$$

Identify any local maxima or minima.

## Theory

A local extremum occurs at a critical point, where the derivative is zero or undefined.

For a quadratic function

$$
f(x) = ax^2 + bx + c
$$

the graph is a parabola. If $a > 0$, the parabola opens upward and has a minimum. If $a < 0$, it opens downward and has a maximum.

The critical point can be found by solving

$$
f'(x) = 0
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

Differentiate the function:

$$
f(x) = 3x^2 - 12x + 7
$$

$$
f'(x) = 6x - 12
$$

Set the derivative equal to zero:

$$
6x - 12 = 0
$$

$$
6x = 12
$$

$$
x = 2
$$

Now calculate the function value at $x = 2$:

$$
f(2) = 3(2)^2 - 12(2) + 7
$$

$$
f(2) = 3 \cdot 4 - 24 + 7
$$

$$
f(2) = 12 - 24 + 7
$$

$$
f(2) = -5
$$

To classify the critical point, use the second derivative:

$$
f''(x) = 6
$$

Since

$$
f''(x) > 0
$$

the critical point is a local minimum.

## Final Result

The function has a local minimum at

$$
x = 2
$$

with value

$$
f(2) = -5
$$

So the minimum point is

$$
(2, -5)
$$

The function has no local maximum.

## Interpretation

The graph is an upward-opening parabola, so it decreases until $x = 2$ and increases after that point. The point $(2, -5)$ is the lowest point of the curve.
