# Task 05 - Trigonometric Components of a Vector

## Problem Statement

A vector $\vec{A}$ has magnitude $15$ and makes an angle of $60^\circ$ with the horizontal axis. Calculate its horizontal and vertical components.

## Theory

If a vector of magnitude $A$ makes an angle $\theta$ with the positive horizontal axis, then its components are

$$
A_x = A \cos \theta
$$

and

$$
A_y = A \sin \theta
$$

These formulas follow from right-triangle trigonometry.

## Step-by-Step Solution

Given:

$$
A = 15
$$

$$
\theta = 60^\circ
$$

### Horizontal Component

$$
A_x = A \cos \theta
$$

$$
A_x = 15 \cos 60^\circ
$$

Since

$$
\cos 60^\circ = \frac{1}{2}
$$

it follows that

$$
A_x = 15 \cdot \frac{1}{2}
$$

$$
A_x = 7.5
$$

### Vertical Component

$$
A_y = A \sin \theta
$$

$$
A_y = 15 \sin 60^\circ
$$

Since

$$
\sin 60^\circ = \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}
$$

it follows that

$$
A_y = 15 \cdot \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}
$$

$$
A_y = \frac{15\sqrt{3}}{2}
$$

Numerically,

$$
A_y \approx 12.99
$$

## Final Result

The vector components are

$$
A_x = 7.5
$$

and

$$
A_y = \frac{15\sqrt{3}}{2} \approx 12.99
$$

## Interpretation

Because the vector makes a $60^\circ$ angle with the horizontal in the first quadrant, both components are positive. The vertical component is larger than the horizontal one, which is consistent with a direction closer to vertical than to horizontal.
