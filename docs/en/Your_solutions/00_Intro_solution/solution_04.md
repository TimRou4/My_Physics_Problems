# Task 04 - Rearranging the Pendulum Formula

## Problem Statement

The formula for the period of a simple pendulum is

$$
T = 2\pi \sqrt{\frac{L}{g}}
$$

Rearrange the equation to obtain a formula for $g$.

## Theory

To isolate a variable in a formula, algebraic operations must be applied to both sides in a valid sequence.

Here the unknown $g$ is inside a square root, so the expression must first be simplified and then squared.

## Step-by-Step Solution

Start from

$$
T = 2\pi \sqrt{\frac{L}{g}}
$$

Divide both sides by $2\pi$:

$$
\frac{T}{2\pi} = \sqrt{\frac{L}{g}}
$$

Square both sides:

$$
\left(\frac{T}{2\pi}\right)^2 = \frac{L}{g}
$$

Rewrite the left-hand side:

$$
\frac{T^2}{4\pi^2} = \frac{L}{g}
$$

Multiply both sides by $g$:

$$
g \frac{T^2}{4\pi^2} = L
$$

Now multiply both sides by $\frac{4\pi^2}{T^2}$:

$$
g = L \cdot \frac{4\pi^2}{T^2}
$$

Therefore,

$$
g = \frac{4\pi^2 L}{T^2}
$$

## Final Result

The rearranged formula is

$$
g = \frac{4\pi^2 L}{T^2}
$$

## Interpretation

This form is useful in experiments. If the pendulum length $L$ and period $T$ are measured, the local gravitational acceleration can be calculated directly.
