# Task 09 - Momentum Comparison

## Problem Statement

Compare the momenta of:

1. A fly of mass $2$ grams moving at $10 \text{ m/s}$.
2. A tennis ball of mass $60$ grams moving at $1 \text{ m/s}$.

Determine which has the greater momentum.

## Theory

Linear momentum is defined by

$$
p = mv
$$

where

- $m$ is the mass,
- $v$ is the speed.

The comparison will be made using the magnitudes of the momenta.

## Step-by-Step Solution

### 1. Convert masses to SI units

For the fly:

$$
2 \text{ g} = 0.002 \text{ kg}
$$

For the tennis ball:

$$
60 \text{ g} = 0.060 \text{ kg}
$$

### 2. Compute the momentum of the fly

$$
p_{\text{fly}} = mv = 0.002 \cdot 10
$$

$$
p_{\text{fly}} = 0.020 \text{ kg m/s}
$$

### 3. Compute the momentum of the tennis ball

$$
p_{\text{ball}} = mv = 0.060 \cdot 1
$$

$$
p_{\text{ball}} = 0.060 \text{ kg m/s}
$$

### 4. Compare

$$
0.060 > 0.020
$$

Therefore,

$$
p_{\text{ball}} > p_{\text{fly}}
$$

The ratio is

$$
\frac{p_{\text{ball}}}{p_{\text{fly}}} = \frac{0.060}{0.020} = 3
$$

## Final Result

The tennis ball has the greater momentum.

Its momentum is

$$
0.060 \text{ kg m/s}
$$

while the fly's momentum is

$$
0.020 \text{ kg m/s}
$$

The tennis ball's momentum is three times larger.

## Interpretation

Although the fly moves much faster, its mass is very small. Momentum depends on both mass and speed, and in this comparison the tennis ball's much larger mass dominates. Therefore the tennis ball carries the larger momentum even at the lower speed.
