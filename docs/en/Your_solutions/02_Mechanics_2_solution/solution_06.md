# Task 06 - Energy Dissipation

## Problem Statement

A tennis ball is dropped from a height of $2.0 \, \mathrm{m}$. After each bounce, it loses $30\%$ of its mechanical energy.

Determine the height reached after the second bounce.

## Theory

At the top of each bounce, the mechanical energy is purely gravitational:

$$
E = mgh
$$

If the ball retains $70\%$ of its energy after each bounce, then the height after each bounce is also multiplied by $0.70$, because $m$ and $g$ remain constant.

Thus, if the ball starts from height $h_0$, then:

$$
h_1 = 0.70 h_0
$$

$$
h_2 = 0.70 h_1 = 0.70^2 h_0
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

Given:
- initial height $h_0 = 2.0 \, \mathrm{m}$
- retained energy fraction after each bounce: $0.70$

### 1. Height after the first bounce

$$
h_1 = 0.70 h_0
$$

$$
h_1 = 0.70 \cdot 2.0 = 1.4 \, \mathrm{m}
$$

### 2. Height after the second bounce

$$
h_2 = 0.70 h_1
$$

$$
h_2 = 0.70 \cdot 1.4 = 0.98 \, \mathrm{m}
$$

Equivalently,

$$
h_2 = 0.70^2 \cdot 2.0 = 0.49 \cdot 2.0 = 0.98 \, \mathrm{m}
$$

## Final Result

The ball rises to

$$
h_2 = 0.98 \, \mathrm{m}
$$

after the second bounce.

## Interpretation

Since the ball loses the same fraction of energy after each bounce, the rebound heights form a geometric sequence. The second rebound height is less than half the original drop height, which illustrates how repeated fractional energy loss quickly reduces the motion.
