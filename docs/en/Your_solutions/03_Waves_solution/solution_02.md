# Task 02 - String Harmonics

## Problem Statement

A guitar string is $64 \, \mathrm{cm}$ long and has a fundamental frequency of $330 \, \mathrm{Hz}$.

Determine the speed of the wave on the string.

## Theory

For a string fixed at both ends, the fundamental mode has one antinode and satisfies

$$
L = \frac{\lambda}{2}
$$

Therefore,

$$
\lambda = 2L
$$

Once the wavelength is known, the wave speed is found from

$$
v = f \lambda
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

### 1. Convert the length to meters

$$
L = 64 \, \mathrm{cm} = 0.64 \, \mathrm{m}
$$

### 2. Determine the wavelength of the fundamental mode

For the fundamental harmonic,

$$
\lambda = 2L
$$

Thus,

$$
\lambda = 2 \cdot 0.64 = 1.28 \, \mathrm{m}
$$

### 3. Determine the wave speed

Use

$$
v = f \lambda
$$

Substitute the values:

$$
v = 330 \cdot 1.28
$$

$$
v = 422.4 \, \mathrm{m/s}
$$

## Final Result

The wave speed on the string is

$$
v = 422.4 \, \mathrm{m/s}
$$

## Interpretation

The fundamental vibration mode on a fixed string contains half of a wavelength along the string length. After determining that wavelength, the wave speed follows directly from the basic wave relation $v = f \lambda$.
