# Task 04 - Phase Difference

## Problem Statement

Determine the phase difference in radians between two points on a wave separated by a distance of $\lambda/3$.

## Theory

The phase difference $\Delta \phi$ between two points separated by a distance $\Delta x$ on a wave is

$$
\Delta \phi = k \Delta x
$$

where the wave number is

$$
k = \frac{2\pi}{\lambda}
$$

Therefore,

$$
\Delta \phi = \frac{2\pi}{\lambda}\Delta x
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

The separation is

$$
\Delta x = \frac{\lambda}{3}
$$

Substitute into the phase relation:

$$
\Delta \phi = \frac{2\pi}{\lambda} \cdot \frac{\lambda}{3}
$$

The wavelength cancels:

$$
\Delta \phi = \frac{2\pi}{3}
$$

## Final Result

The phase difference is

$$
\Delta \phi = \frac{2\pi}{3} \, \mathrm{rad}
$$

## Interpretation

A distance of one full wavelength corresponds to a phase change of $2\pi$ radians. Therefore, a distance of one third of a wavelength corresponds to one third of $2\pi$, which is $2\pi/3$ radians.
