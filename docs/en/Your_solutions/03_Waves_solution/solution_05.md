# Task 05 - Echo Ranging

## Problem Statement

A person shouts towards a cliff and hears the echo $1 \, \mathrm{s}$ later. The speed of sound in air is $343 \, \mathrm{m/s}$.

Determine the distance to the cliff.

## Theory

An echo involves a round trip:

1. the sound travels from the person to the cliff,
2. the reflected sound returns from the cliff to the person.

Thus, the total measured time corresponds to twice the one-way distance.

If $d$ is the distance to the cliff, then

$$
2d = vt
$$

so

$$
d = \frac{vt}{2}
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

Given:

$$
v = 343 \, \mathrm{m/s}
$$

$$
t = 1 \, \mathrm{s}
$$

Use the formula:

$$
d = \frac{vt}{2}
$$

Substitute the values:

$$
d = \frac{343 \cdot 1}{2}
$$

$$
d = 171.5 \, \mathrm{m}
$$

## Final Result

The cliff is

$$
171.5 \, \mathrm{m}
$$

away.

## Interpretation

The measured $1 \, \mathrm{s}$ is not the travel time to the cliff alone. It includes both the forward path and the return path, so the one-way distance is half of the total sound path.
