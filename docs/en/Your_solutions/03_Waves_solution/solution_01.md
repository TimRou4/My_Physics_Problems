# Task 01 - Wave Properties

## Problem Statement

A sound wave in air has a frequency of $440 \, \mathrm{Hz}$. The speed of sound in air is $343 \, \mathrm{m/s}$.

Determine:

1. the wavelength in air,
2. the wavelength in water, where the speed of sound is $1482 \, \mathrm{m/s}$.

## Theory

For a wave, the relation between wave speed $v$, frequency $f$, and wavelength $\lambda$ is

$$
v = f \lambda
$$

Therefore,

$$
\lambda = \frac{v}{f}
$$

When a wave passes from one medium to another, its frequency remains the same, while its speed and wavelength may change.

## Step-by-Step Solution

### 1. Wavelength in air

Using

$$
\lambda_{\text{air}} = \frac{v_{\text{air}}}{f}
$$

Substitute the data:

$$
\lambda_{\text{air}} = \frac{343}{440}
$$

$$
\lambda_{\text{air}} \approx 0.7795 \, \mathrm{m}
$$

### 2. Wavelength in water

The frequency remains

$$
f = 440 \, \mathrm{Hz}
$$

The speed in water is

$$
v_{\text{water}} = 1482 \, \mathrm{m/s}
$$

Thus,

$$
\lambda_{\text{water}} = \frac{v_{\text{water}}}{f}
$$

$$
\lambda_{\text{water}} = \frac{1482}{440}
$$

$$
\lambda_{\text{water}} \approx 3.368 \, \mathrm{m}
$$

## Final Result

The wavelength in air is

$$
\lambda_{\text{air}} \approx 0.78 \, \mathrm{m}
$$

The wavelength in water is

$$
\lambda_{\text{water}} \approx 3.37 \, \mathrm{m}
$$

## Interpretation

Because sound travels much faster in water than in air, the wavelength is much larger in water. The frequency does not change when the wave enters a new medium, so the change in speed is reflected directly in the wavelength.
